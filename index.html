Artsy Shmup

A shmup in space where you jump platforms and blast baddies only to reveal it was all an escapist fantasy in the mind of a scared kid

Game Concept

A 2D shmup in space where in each level you progress by moving from platform to platform (discrete movement) and fighting off waves of enemies with shooting controlled by the mouse in all directions. Initially the visual aesthetic will be realistic and gradually become more stylistic, simulating the escapist fantasy of a child in an awkward social situation as revealed in the final level.

Game Play Draft

Each level contains 'platforms' which allow the player to move in discrete units towards the end of the level. At each platform the player must defeat a wave of enemies before the next platform becomes available for him to move to. The player can move backwards to a limit of 5 platforms to prevent excessive backtracking. The objective is to reach the last platform of the level and defeat the last wave of enemies. The game is set in space with the character as an astronaut shooting off enemies with a laser weapon. The mouse can be used to aim the laser weapon in any direction around the player, and enemies in the waves can fly in at the player from all directions. The key actions are move and shoot and the mechanics are kept as simple as possible to allow players to focus on the artistic elements of the game.

As said before, the game is set in space but the physics are different: the player’s character uses gravity and his/her discrete movements are go forward/backwards and jump. However, gravity does not affect the enemies the player must defeat.

Mechanics

The player, who is affected by gravity unlike the enemies, moves and jumps from platform to platform in space. Normal enemies are spawned around him and move towards the player to hurt him. Shooting enemies are spawned around him and shoot at him but they don't move. Phantom enemies are spawned on random platforms and don't harm the player, they just block the path and make the player face other enemies while trying to kill the phantom enemy.

Different enemies are introduced in different levels.

The height the player can reach jumping from platform to platform changes from platform to platform and is random, so the platforms are spawned at random positions related to the height the player can reach.

The player moves and jumps using the arrow keys or the keys A, W, D. The player can pause the game pressing the space bar, and resume pressing the same key again.

The player will die if he falls between platforms. Enemies and player have a specific number of lives and will die when they run out of them. To avoid dying, the player must shoot at the enemies until they are dead. Killing enemies will reward the player with points (different amount of points depending on the type of enemy).

The player starts with 3 lives and every time a new level is reached, the number of lives will increase by 1. There is a specific number of enemies of the same type that can be alive at the same time. When the player kills an enemy, a new enemy of the same kind will be spawned shortly after.

To aim, the user can use the mouse. To shoot, the left mouse button must be pressed. The player has unlimited number of bullets.

The player jumps from platform to platform moving right to recover a lost object. Once he gets it, he must go back moving left. When he's about to reach the final portal, a boss enemy will be spawned. The boss enemy shoots multiple bullets at the same time and the player must shoot him until he's dead.

Between levels there are intermediate dialogue scenes where the player has a conversation with Mr Anubis. To load the next dialogue in the conversation, the NEXT button on the bottom of the screen must be pressed.

There is a cheating system that can be used a set number of times in each level but it's not explained to the player.
